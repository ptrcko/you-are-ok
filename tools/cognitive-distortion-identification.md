<section>
  <h2>Cognitive Distortion Identification</h2>
  <p>This tool helps you notice patterns in thinking by referencing a fixed list of cognitive distortions.</p>
  <p class="notice">Purpose: offer definitions you can apply to your own text; no scores or correctness checks are involved.</p>
</section>

<section>
  <h2>What it is for</h2>
  <ul>
    <li>Pairing your own words with neutral distortion descriptions.</li>
    <li>Exploring patterns without changing the original entry.</li>
  </ul>
</section>

<section>
  <h2>What it is not for</h2>
  <ul>
    <li>Labelling people or issuing recommendations.</li>
    <li>Tracking progress, achievements, or compliance.</li>
  </ul>
</section>

<section>
  <h2>Distortion reference</h2>
  <p>Use these definitions as a checklist. Apply any that fit your thought; you can choose more than one.</p>
  <dl class="definition-list">
    <dt>All-or-Nothing Thinking (AON)</dt>
    <dd>Viewing situations in black-and-white categories without room for middle ground.</dd>

    <dt>Overgeneralization (OG)</dt>
    <dd>Drawing broad conclusions from a single event or limited evidence.</dd>

    <dt>Mental Filter (MF)</dt>
    <dd>Focusing on a single negative detail while ignoring the broader context.</dd>

    <dt>Discounting the Positive (DP)</dt>
    <dd>Rejecting or downplaying positive experiences or feedback.</dd>

    <dt>Jumping to Conclusions (JC)</dt>
    <dd>Assuming outcomes or intentions without clear evidence.</dd>

    <dt>Mind-Reading (MR)</dt>
    <dd>Believing you know what others are thinking without checking.</dd>

    <dt>Fortune-Telling (FT)</dt>
    <dd>Predicting future events as facts without supporting data.</dd>

    <dt>Magnification and Minimization (MM)</dt>
    <dd>Exaggerating negatives or shrinking positives out of proportion.</dd>

    <dt>Emotional Reasoning (ER)</dt>
    <dd>Treating feelings as evidence that something is true.</dd>

    <dt>Should Statements (SH)</dt>
    <dd>Applying rigid rules or expectations to yourself or others.</dd>

    <dt>Labeling (LAB)</dt>
    <dd>Assigning fixed, global labels to yourself or others.</dd>

    <dt>Blame (BL)</dt>
    <dd>Assigning fault without considering complexity or shared factors.</dd>

    <dt>Self-Blame (SB)</dt>
    <dd>Holding yourself solely responsible for events outside your control.</dd>

    <dt>Other-Blame (OB)</dt>
    <dd>Holding others solely responsible without acknowledging any nuance.</dd>
  </dl>
</section>
